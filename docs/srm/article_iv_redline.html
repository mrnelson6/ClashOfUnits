Section 3.    Constraints on SRMs.

An SRM not involving "points per yard" may increase or decrease by one (1) the current value (see Appendix "A") of one Roster Setting (i.e., the number of players at a particular position) or Scoring Setting (i.e., the points awarded for a particular occurrence in NFL games). by the SRM Increment specified for that setting in Appendix "A."  Where no SRM Increment is specified, the default increment of one (1) shall apply for Roster Settings, and the provisions of Sections 3.1 and 3.2 shall apply for Scoring Settings.

    Section 3.1.    Points per Yard.Weighted SRM Increments.

An SRM may increase or decrease only by 0.1 any Scoring Setting related to "points per yard."

Each Scoring Setting shall have an SRM Increment (denoted with the ± symbol in Appendix "A") that reflects the frequency-weighted impact of that setting on League scoring.  Settings that occur infrequently in NFL games (e.g., 200+ Yard Rushing Game) shall carry a larger SRM Increment than settings that occur frequently (e.g., Rush Attempt), such that each SRM produces a comparable effect on total League scoring regardless of the category selected.

    Section 3.2.    Specified Points.Determination of SRM Increments.

Notwithstanding the above, if a Scoring Setting has a specified SRM interval (see Appendix "A"), such interval is applicable for any SRM to that Scoring Setting.

SRM Increments shall be determined by historical analysis of NFL regular season data, categorizing each Scoring Setting into an impact tier based on its frequency of occurrence multiplied by its base adjustment.  The Commissioner may, by Vote or by reasonable exercise of discretion, update the SRM Increments to reflect changes in NFL trends, provided that the methodology and data used are made available to all Managers.  The current SRM Increments, their underlying methodology, and interactive tools for review are published at clashofunits.ttnelson.com/srm.

    Section 3.3.    Roster Settings.

An SRM to a Roster Setting (i.e., the number of players at a particular position) may increase or decrease by one (1) the current value of that setting.

Sections 1, 2, 4, 5, and 6 are unchanged.
